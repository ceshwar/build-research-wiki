# The Language of Approval

> CHI 2026: causal + predictive study of what linguistic attributes drive upvotes and awards on Reddit, plus a guideline audit revealing a policy-practice gap.

## Summary

While [[positive-reinforcement-reddit]] shows that reward changes behavior, this paper asks what post attributes *earn* that reward. On 11 million posts across 100 subreddits, the authors combine quasi-experimental causal inference with predictive modeling, controlling for author reputation, timing, and community context. Clear writing and broad help requests increase odds of high scores; complicated language, tentativeness, and toxicity reduce them. Models trained on curated features detect highly-upvoted posts with high AUC. An audit of community guidelines finds most rules focus on civility and formatting — not the attributes that actually drive approval.

## Key takeaways

- **Linguistic features** have identifiable causal effects on community reward signals.
- **Policy-practice gap**: written rules under-specify what earns positive feedback.
- Supports **interfaces** that teach desirable contribution style, not only prohibition lists.
