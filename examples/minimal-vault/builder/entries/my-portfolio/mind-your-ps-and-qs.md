## Abstract

Online communities are constantly growing, with dozens of platforms housing millions of users. Large and small communities alike rely on volunteer moderators to maintain order. Despite their key role, moderators are given a toolbox of punishments and asked to fend off barrages of harmful content. However, prior research shows that positive feedback may proactively encourage higher quality contributions and discourage norm violations. Moreover, moderators themselves have requested support for locating and rewarding content to encourage in their communities. These requests notwithstanding, there is a tangible lack of practical support through tools. Building off moderators’ ideas, we build a novel moderation system, the Positive Queue, that augments Reddit’s existing moderator interface with features to discover and reward desirable content. Through a user study of moderators, we find that the system has value to vastly different moderation settings. As a form of positive reinforcement, the principle of encouraging desirable outcomes by introducing positive stimuli [23], positive feedback may actually increase user motivation [44] and enable communities to better communicate norms [40]. Notably, moderators recognize the power positive reinforcement, but cite challenges with actually finding content worthy of receiving rewards in their spaces. In addition, moderators who reported not attempting any form of positive reinforcement strategies explained that they were deterred by the lack of available tools [45]. Other tools developed by HCI researchers to proactively moderate communities have been effective at identifying and triaging comments for moderators [10] and encouraging users to reflect before contributing to tension online [14]. Similarly, existing research shows that positive feedback, even given out anonymously through upvotes, can be effective at encouraging better behavior by the recipient going forward [47].
